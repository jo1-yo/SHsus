Coordinated Clubs & Programs

We coordinated 10 student clubs, including the Loomberg Finance Club and PEC Economics Club, to jointly organize lectures, workshops, Model UN conferences, and interdisciplinary forums on topics spanning economics, environment, culture, and society. By aligning the activities and schedules of these clubs, we facilitated resource sharing and collaborative management, fostering interdisciplinary learning and building a sustainable community for over 300 students.

Herwild

Herwild is a student-led cultural revitalization group empowering rural Chinese women artisans. Initially focused on minority artisans in Gang Village, Anhui, we later expanded to support artisans across Hebei, Yunnan, and other regions in rural China. We provided access to formal business training and mentorship, helping these artisans translate traditional skills into modern enterprises. Through our initiatives, we promoted the growth of rural communities while bringing Chinese cultural heritage to major cities across the country.

Sparksight

Sparksight is a major annual branded event at Shanghai Pinghe School. Each year, we invite six students and one teacher to deliver theatrical-style talks. This year's theme was the SDGs, with each speaker exploring sustainable development issues such as gender equality and poverty, drawing on personal experiences or current news events. The talks were insightful, engaging, and thought-provoking, attracting multiple experts and the school principal as supporting guests.

Public Engagement Project

A campus-wide initiative linking sustainability and cultural heritage through public participation. The Charity Fair showcased 100% recyclable products and cultural designs co-created with the Palace Museum Creative Line. While the Sustainable Board Games transformed environmental topics into interactive learning. Together, these activities raised over $8,500 and strengthened students' commitment to eco-conscious and culturally rooted action.
